# NNNN — Short title in sentence case

* **Status:** Proposed | Accepted | Deprecated | Superseded by NNNN
* **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

## Context

The forces at play — what's true about the system, what's painful, what
prompted this decision now. Keep it specific. If a future reader can't tell
which constraint led where, the ADR isn't doing its job.

## Decision

What we picked, stated as a single declarative sentence followed by the
shape of the change. Decisions are imperative ("**We will use…**"), not
discursive ("we considered…").

## Consequences

* **Good:** what gets easier.
* **Bad:** what gets harder, the maintenance cost.
* **Follow-ups:** any decision deferred, work this opens up, or risks to
  watch.

## Alternatives considered

Bullet form — option name + one-line "why not." Keep this short; the goal
isn't to defend the decision, it's to save the next reader from
re-deriving the trade-offs.
